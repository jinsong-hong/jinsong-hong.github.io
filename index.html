Research Focus

Robust locomotion through control-aware learning.

My research interest is to make legged robots more robust in real contact-rich environments. I am especially interested in controllers that combine physical structure with learning, rather than treating reinforcement learning and classical control as separate tools.

  • Sim-to-real reinforcement learning for legged robots
  • Dynamics-based low-level control and actuator-aware interfaces
  • Disturbance observer and force observer based control
  • Admittance and impedance control for physical interaction
  • Robustness evaluation under delay, contact, and external disturbance

Research Directions

What I am building toward

01

Observer-Augmented Locomotion

Using DOB/FOB estimates to expose unmodeled dynamics and contact disturbance to low-level controllers and learned policies.

02

Control-Aware Sim-to-Real RL

Studying how action space, actuator model, delay, domain randomization, and safety layers affect hardware transfer.

03

Compliant Physical Interaction

Comparing position-target policies, torque-level policies, impedance control, and admittance control under matched disturbance tests.

Projects

Main Project

Observer-Aware Sim-to-Real Locomotion

My current work focuses on how dynamics-based low-level control can be combined with learned locomotion policies. The goal is to improve robustness under actuator mismatch, contact uncertainty, delay, and external disturbance without relying only on reward tuning.

  • Disturbance and force observer based feedback signals
  • Admittance/impedance interfaces for contact-rich walking
  • Policy action-space comparison: torque vs. position target
  • Hardware validation with matched disturbance tests

Parallel-Link Legged Robot Control

Design Project

Parallel-Link Quadruped Robot Design

This project studies the mechanical structure of a parallel-link quadruped robot platform. The design emphasizes compact actuation, load-bearing leg geometry, manufacturable components, and structural safety under representative load cases.

Method CAD modeling and finite element analysis
Load Case 400 N structural load evaluation
Components Motor housing, ring structure, and leg link
